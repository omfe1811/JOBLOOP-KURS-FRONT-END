Frida Kahlo

Surrealism

Frida Kahlo stands as one of the most famous women artists of all time. She is remembered for her self-portraits, pain and passion, and bold, vibrant colors. She is celebrated in Mexico for her attention to Mexican and indigenous culture and by feminists for her depiction of the female experience and form

Tamara de Lempicka

Art Deco

With works that exuded cool elegance and transgressive sensuality, Tamara de Lempicka (1894–1980) helped define Art Deco. Her paintings captured the glamour and vitality of postwar Paris and the cosmopolitan sheen of Hollywood celebrity. As the portraitist of the 1920s bohemian Paris elite, she depicted the illustrious and notorious figures from the between-the-wars avant-garde idyll on the Left Bank. She was a seductress of men and women alike and, by her own admission, chose to live in the margins, beyond the “normal rules of society”.

Georgia O’Keeffe

Modernism

Georgia O’Keeffe is one of the most significant artists of the 20th century, renowned for her contribution to modern art. One of the first female painters to achieve worldwide acclaim from critics and the general public, Georgia O'Keeffe was an American painter who over the span of seven decades created innovative impressionist images that challenged perceptions and evolved constantly throughout her career.
